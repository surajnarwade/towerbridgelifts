What is Tower Bridge?

The Iconic London Landmark

Tower Bridge is a world-famous symbol of London. Built between 1886 and 1894, it is a combination of a bascule (drawbridge) and a suspension bridge. It's often mistaken for its neighbor, London Bridge, but its distinctive Victorian Gothic towers make it unmistakable.

What is a Bridge Lift?

The bridge lifts its massive bascules to allow large vessels to pass through. Any vessel over 9 meters tall can request a bridge lift. This historic tradition continues today, and it's a sight to behold for tourists and locals alike.

Fun Fact: The bascules are raised about 800 times a year!

Historic & Funny Moments

  • The Flying Bus (1952): A number 78 double-decker bus was on the bridge when it started to lift. The driver, Albert Gunter, famously accelerated and jumped the three-foot gap to safety! He was rewarded with a £10 bonus.
  • Trapping the President (1997): The bridge once split Bill Clinton's presidential motorcade in half. The US Secret Service wasn't happy, but the bridge follows the law: River traffic has priority!
  • The Hawkeyed Pilot (1912): A pilot named Frank McClean had to fly his biplane between the bascules and the walkways when he found himself too low to fly over.
